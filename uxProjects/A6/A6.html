A6 - Molding and Casting Part 2




Silicone Mold


I already printed the mold of molds for this project so my next step was making the silicone mold. I used Oomoo 30, and before I mixed A and B together, I measured the volume of my 3D printed mold with rice to figure out how much Oomoo I needed (Thanks to Vanessa for that idea).

I also used a bit of vegetable oil on my 3D printed mold because I was nervous about the silicone sticking.

I mixed A and B together, poured it into my mold, and let it cure for about 8 hours.

Once I felt it was ready, I used a small knife to separate the silicone from the walls of the mold and it came out pretty easily! It turned out well, the thickness was a good amount (not flimsy), and the 2 sides fit together


Casting

I mixed the Hydro Stone with water until I received a crepe batter consistency and poured it into the mold. I used some spoons to hold the two sides together to prevent bowing.

Once the piece hardened and I took it out, I noticed there was an air pocket at the top. I tried casting it again and pouring more of the solution into the mold, but the next one also turned out to have an air pocket.

Luckily I noticed a comment from Junchao on my last assignment, and he mentioned tilting the silicone mold while casting the plaster to ensure there would be no air. I tried that and there was no air pocket this time!


Final

Here are my final 4 casted pieces. I sanded them down to make them look more uniform.
