A3 - Getting Started with 3D Modeling



Assembling the 3D Printer

For this portion I don’t have many photos because I set up my printer 2 weeks ago but it took me a few hours to put everything together. The instructions that were provided with my Ender 3 weren’t super informative, only providing images, so a few steps on the instructions weren’t clear to understand. Here are some pictures that I managed to take while I was assembling the 3D printer.


Adjusting Extruder’s nuts

My extruder’s nuts weren’t loose when I assembled my printer, so this was something I didn’t have to do. I still double checked if they were tight enough.


Checking for Loose Screws

Overall, I made sure to tighten all the screws on my printer. A few screws loosened up later during my print, but I’ll get into that in a bit.


Other Problems while assembling

The belt on the x-axis wasn’t tight, so when I tried turning on my machine and pressing the Auto Home setting, I would get a “Homing Failed” please reset printer error. My friend Kaitlyn helped me tighten the belt along the x-axis.


Adjusting the Build Plate Level

This step was by far the most frustrating for me because I spent 2 days trying to adjust the build plate level. I followed the video provided from the slide deck on Canvas, and I downloaded the bed leveling files. Initially my bed was too low, and the extruder was too high, so when I tried to raise the bed, the bottom left adjustment knob would just completely come out. Because of this, I adjusted the z-limit switch to be lower, so the extruder would reach closer to the bed, which seemed to work better; I was able to adjust the knobs properly.

Initially my filament wasn’t properly adhering to the bed. When I tightened the knobs to raise the bed, then the extruder was too close. There was a lot of trial and error and minute adjustments on the knobs and the Z-limit switch to finally level the bed.

My filament bowden tube was loose, and I had to change my filament (I used the provided filament from Creality initially) so my filament wasn’t coming out properly. However, I finally managed to level my bed after some time.

Cylinders

When I started printing cylinders, that is where I faced more problems. For the first cylinder, my printer started printing spaghetti printing. I tried printing my other cylinder first, and it was fine so I figured it wasn’t an issue with the bed level but it could have been an issue with the gcode file. After I recreated the file, the cylinder printed normally. I didn't manage to take a picture of my Rhino model but the cylinder is the same as the cylinder on the side without the supports.

Nested Object

In the end, it turned out really nicely, and I thought it was cute!
